ELIPSS Portal

Welcome to ELIPSS. This web portal provides visualization and processing tools for data created during the Unmanned Aerial Systems class in spring 2018, held at the Institute for Geoinformatics in the University of Münster. The course was held by Professor Jan Lehmann, Professor Torsten Prinz and Professor Sergey Mukhametov.

The main objective of the project was the development and application of exploratory approaches using several geoespatial technologies to monitor the renaturation process in the Aa River, located in city of Münster, Germany.

The data displayed is a result of extensive joint work. Our class was divided in multiple project groups: Esri Cloud, Lidar, Image Analysis, Project Management, Sensor Platforms, SenseBox, Web Portal. More information regarding individual group objectives can be found here.

Renaturating the Aa River

Greater awareness and actions has been developed in recent decades by trans-national governmental institutions from national to local level, intending to perform renaturation of rivers under the European Union water policies entitled: EU Water Framework Directive (WFD) with “good quantitative status” and “good chemical status” of all rivers until 2023.
Major challenges that rivers and lakes in European cities face are grouped into: water availability and supply challenges, water quality issues, structural changes to and physical modifications of rivers and lakes (EU, 2016).

The Aa river located in the South of Münster, in the year 2008, has undergone a process of renaturation by the municipal government. In order to restore to the natural form of this river.

Therefore, perform monitoring and exploratory approaches to assess the effects on vegetation and hydromorphological quality such as river bank structure, river continuity and/ or substrate of the river bed, turned out to be a suitable and assertive opportunity to gain knowledge with the application of geospatial technologies to practical contexts.
